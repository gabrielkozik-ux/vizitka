AI Philosophy in Law: Why Not to Fear Synergy

Published: March 12, 2026 | Author: Mgr. Gabriel Kožík

People often ask me if I am not afraid that one day an algorithm will replace me as a lawyer. I answer no. Machines do not have empathy or strategic thinking, which are critical for solving complex legal disputes. Ever since I started experimenting with the first chatbots in December 2022 and later (from February 2024) began using Google AI Studio and Gemini daily, my work has changed fundamentally. I don't work less – I work differently and with much more depth.

Interestingly, the greatest strength of AI does not lie in writing contracts, but in interpretation and finding connections. I do not use technology as automatic text generators, but as sparring partners to analyze complicated legal issues.

Why Synergy?

For example, when I recently needed to quickly map the development of case law on liability for custom software defects, AI in Google AI Studio analyzed hundreds of pages of Supreme Court rulings and extracted the key argument lines within ten seconds. As a lawyer, I would have spent the entire weekend on that. I can invest the saved time in what the client truly needs: devising business strategies and personal negotiation.

My philosophy of AI integration is built on several principles. First and foremost, it is about understanding the client's context, not mechanically copying paragraphs. AI allows me to go below the surface and find connections that might escape the human eye under time pressure. Furthermore, it is about surgical precision in routine work and eliminating errors when reviewing extensive documents. However, the most important point is the Human-in-the-loop approach – I personally review and complement every sentence the system proposes with my expertise. I stand behind the output, not the machine.

In practice, this means that since the end of 2025, I have been intensively building custom agentic systems and automating routine administration in the office. For me, law is not a static text, but a dynamic problem-solving process.

The barrier between technology and traditional law practice has fallen. Anyone who does not understand this and continues to spend dozens of hours mechanically rewriting templates will soon lose competitiveness in the modern business market. Artificial intelligence will not replace lawyers, but lawyers with AI will replace those without it.
