AI Philosophy in Law: Why Not to Fear Synergy

Published: March 12, 2026 | Author: Mgr. Gabriel Kožík

I am a lawyer who is not afraid to use AI to streamline processes, improve quality, and reduce the cost of my services. This sentence often raises questions: Will machines replace us? Will the human dimension be lost from law? My experience says the exact opposite.

I have been working with AI chatbots since December 2022, and with Google AI tools (Gemini, NotebookLM, AI Studio) on a daily basis since February 2024. During this time, I have developed a philosophy that does not pit law and technology against each other, but rather brings them into synergy.

Why Synergy?

The legal profession and AI should work together to ensure better interpretation of legal norms and a deeper understanding of client requirements. AI doesn't write the law for me – AI helps me see connections that might escape the human eye and deliver surgically precise outputs.

My vision is built on five key pillars:

  1. Better Interpretation of Norms: AI can analyze thousands of pages of case law and texts in seconds, providing me with a broader context for decision-making.
  2. Understanding the Client: Thanks to more efficient analysis of source materials, I can focus more on what the client truly needs, not just what the law says.
  3. Surgical Precision: Minimizing human error in routine tasks and precise formulation are the results of combining my expertise with high-performance machines.
  4. Effective Time Investment: What used to take hours now takes minutes. I dedicate this time to strategic thinking, rather than mechanical drafting.
  5. High Standard of Care (Human-in-the-loop): I always review, edit, and refine all outputs with a high degree of care. I use my expert knowledge to deliver a result that I, as a lawyer, stand behind.

Since December 2025, I have been intensively involved in vibecoding, agentic systems, and workflow automation. I believe that law is not just about sections and paragraphs, but about solving problems. And technology is the best tool to find these solutions faster and with greater certainty.

Integrating AI into work is no longer a choice. It is a necessity for anyone who wants to succeed in modern business and offer services appropriate for the 21st century. The biggest threat is not the technology itself, but the inaction of those who refuse to understand it.
