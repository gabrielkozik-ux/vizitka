How to Safely Master AI in Business

Published: June 3, 2026 | Author: Mgr. Gabriel Kožík

Integrating artificial intelligence into business processes yields a huge competitive advantage and saves time. However, if done blindly, it poses significant legal and security threats. In the new episode of our law firm's podcast, IUSTORIA, we discuss how to manage AI integration safely.

Note: The video podcast is in Czech. An English executive summary of the key takeaways is provided below.

We discuss the practical impacts of European regulations and the necessary steps every business owner or manager should take to mitigate security risks and protect their core IP.

📌 Key Takeaways & Executive Summary

  • EU AI Act Compliance: The European AI regulation is not just for software developers; it applies to all businesses deploying AI. I explain the core concept of risk-based system categorization (prohibited, high-risk, limited) and the resulting compliance duties.
  • The Threat of "Shadow AI": This occurs when employees input sensitive client data, internal source code, or company trade secrets into unauthorized public AI chatbots. These inputs can be used to train public models, risking confidential data leaks.
  • AI Audit as the First Step: Before establishing policies, companies must conduct an audit to map out which AI tools their employees are actually using, for what purposes, and with what types of data.
  • Internal AI Policy: Setting up clear guidelines. Employees must know which tools are approved, what data they are permitted to input, and under what conditions.
  • Secure Deployment Pathways: Opting for paid corporate plans with guaranteed data privacy (enterprise licenses), utilizing API endpoints, or deploying self-hosted open-source models (such as Llama) running on company hardware (on-premise).
