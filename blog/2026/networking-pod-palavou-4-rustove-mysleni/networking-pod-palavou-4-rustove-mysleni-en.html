Reportáž: Networking Pod Pálavou #4: Růstové myšlení

Publikováno: 17. 2. 2026 | Autor: Mgr. Gabriel Kožík

Why are we as entrepreneurs so interested in how our children learn? Because a fixed or growth mindset starts as early as kindergarten. At Pod Pálavou, we often discuss that today's goal is not crammed knowledge, but the ability of children (and adults) to work with emotions and cooperate. As Anna Polehlová Jourová says in her lecture: „For our potential to run at full throttle, we must first and foremost feel good.“

When we feel good and don't let the system trample our passion for the journey itself, we gain the courage to find deeper meaning in what we do. In business, just like in upbringing, the key is not to be held back by the past. History may be a great teacher, but it is a lousy driver – especially if we look into it more often than out the windshield. We constantly hear reasons why something cannot be done or why we must be cautious.

But take a look at Estonia. This country started from a much more challenging position, yet managed to digitally and economically transform itself simply by finding the courage to stop being held back by its own past. At the Pod Pálavou club, we believe in a similar principle: tradition should give us roots and stability, not tie our hands when building modern companies. Doing business from the heart does not mean moving slowly, but having the ambition to grow – with respect for the place we live, but without pointless excuses about market specifics.

When these imaginary brakes are released, space opens up for genuine cooperation, which is exactly what we are building at our meetings. As Anna discovered years ago, the most valuable formal contacts often naturally transform into deep personal friendships. „Trust is the hardest currency we have in business,“ was said among other things, and we absolutely resonate with that. In our community, we are not just creating an empty database of contacts, but rather a safe space to share both successes and downfalls.

Because doing business from the heart means knowing that you are not alone, and that you can rely on the people around you even when the storms come. Thank you to all the guests and members for the honesty and energy you bring to every meeting!
